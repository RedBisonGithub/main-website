meet the team

Leadership

Lynn Martin

Lynn Martin is CEO of Red Bison Technology Group, where he is responsible for profitably growing the business by ensuring the company stays ahead of the growing connectivity needs of the commercial real estate market with platform and software solutions that save on capital costs upfront and provide revenue-generating opportunities that create value for owners, tenants, and Red Bison. Previously, Lynn was President of the Commercial Division of GigaSphere, where he led the commercial real estate initiatives of the company. Prior to this role, he served as COO of FTE Networks, where he led its subsidiary, CrossLayer, in rolling out advanced network solutions to a significant portfolio of commercial properties across the United States. Lynn has also held senior executive leadership positions with technology and telecommunications companies, including Nexius, Telcordia Technologies, Accenture, and Level 3 Communications. He attended the University of Idaho, where he pursued a major in architectural engineering with a minor in construction management. Lynn holds three patents: one for network routing, a second for automated installation of service, and a third for network service order entry systems.

CEO

Dave Cahoon

Dave Cahoon is CTO of Red Bison Technology Group, where he collaborates with internal and external resources in defining, building, and delivering multiple networks and security solutions for a broad range of customers at Red Bison. In addition, he is responsible for the overall security and privacy of the business. Dave has more than fifteen years of proven executive leadership experience leading high-performing technical teams. Throughout his career, he has personally led dozens of tactical units with upwards of 150+ employees. Mr. Cahoon’s career includes several other technical leadership, security, and engineering roles for Intel Security, McAfee, and RSA. In addition, he is proud to have worked at NASA’s Langley Research Center in one of the most demanding engineering and network roles available in U.S. government service. He architected the network platform for all of NASA Langley Research Center, which included 165 buildings.

Glen Christofferson

Glen Christofferson is the Chief Financial Officer of Red Bison Technology Group where he oversees the planning, implementation, and management of all finance activities within the company, including budgeting, forecasting, negotiations, and business planning. In his role as CFO, he ensures the company utilizes all of its resources efficiently and complies with all governmental financial regulations. Prior to Red Bison, Glen spent the last 30+ years of his career in broad-based real estate, financial and executive leadership roles, including a founding partner of Global Investing Group (GIG) and Urban Renaissance Group URG). He also held executive leadership positions at Equity Office Properties, Kidder Mathews, Fisher Communications, Seattle Sonics and Weyerhaeuser Real Estate Company (WRECO). Glen holds degrees in Finance, Accounting and Economics from Pacific Lutheran University in addition to CPA and CMA designations.

Julia Pitlik

Julia Pitlik is Chief Marketing Officer of Red Bison Technology Group, where she oversees the development and execution of strategic marketing programs and new market categories that create demand and brand awareness, along with increased revenues and market share for the company. Before Red Bison, Julia was CMO for BeyondEdge; a software-defined edge compute company focused on advancing networks via open and modular software platforms that deliver highly automated in-building networks. Before BeyondEdge, she was SVP of Marketing for CrossLayer, a managed network services company with a software-driven, edge compute platform designed to meet the market needs of commercial real estate owners and developers. During her career, Julia has held several executive and senior management positions in business development, sales, marketing, public relations, and talent acquisition with Nexius, Fujitsu, Sprint, and Texas Instruments. Pitlik is a graduate of the UT Dallas Executive MBA program and received a B.S. (magna cum laude) in Public Relations from Illinois State University. She is actively involved in local and national community and philanthropic programs tied to neurodiversity. She is a member of the Music Movement Board of Directors, a nonprofit organization that develops programs to support those with autism.

E.J. von Schaumburg

E.J. von Schaumburg is Senior Vice President of Partner Alliances at Red Bison Technology Group, where he is developing an ecosystem of partners to add value to Red Bison’s CRE Connectivity, Sustainability, and Security Platform. These partnerships enable Red Bison to deliver significant cost savings, new tenant technologies, and new revenue streams that increase the asset values of commercial properties.
